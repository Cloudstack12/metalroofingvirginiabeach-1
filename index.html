Skylight Installation: The Ultimate Solution for Dark and Dreary Rooms


Read Post

Do you have a room in your home that always seems dark and gloomy, no matter how much you try to brighten it up? Many homeowners struggle with this common problem, leading to a lack of natural light, poor air quality, and a negative impact on mood and well-being. However, there is a simple solution: skylight installation. A skylight to your home can bring in natural light and fresh air while improving energy efficiency and overall aesthetics. In this blog, we will explore the many benefits of skylight installation and provide the information you need to choose the right skylight for your home and ensure a successful installation process.

Benefits of Skylight Installation

Skylight installation is becoming an increasingly popular option for homeowners who want to improve the lighting and ventilation in their homes. Not only does it offer a range of benefits, but it also adds a touch of modern elegance to any living space.

Natural Light: The Ultimate Solution for Dark and Dreary Rooms

One of the main benefits of skylight installation is adding natural light to your home. Natural light not only makes your home look brighter and more welcoming, but it also has several health benefits. Exposure to natural light can help regulate your sleep-wake cycle, boost your mood, and even reduce the risk of seasonal affective disorder (SAD).


In addition to the physical and psychological benefits, natural light can help you save money on your energy bills. By reducing your reliance on electric lighting, you can cut your electricity bill and enjoy a more environmentally friendly home.


Fresh Air: Improving Indoor Air Quality and Circulation

Another critical benefit of skylight installation is improving indoor air quality and circulation. Ventilating skylights, which can be opened and closed, can help to remove stale air and bring in fresh, clean air from outside. This can help reduce the risk of respiratory issues and allergies and improve the overall comfort of your home.


Energy Efficiency: Saving Money on Electricity and Heating Bills

Skylight installation can also help to improve the energy efficiency of your home. By allowing more natural light in, you can reduce your reliance on electric lighting and save money on electricity bills. Additionally, skylights can help regulate your home's temperature, reducing the need for heating and air conditioning. This can help reduce energy consumption and lower heating and cooling bills.


Mood Booster: Improving Mental Health and Well-Being

Finally, skylight installation can be a significant mood booster. Exposure to natural light has been shown to improve mental health and well-being and can even help to reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety. By allowing more natural light into your home, you can create a brighter, uplifting environment conducive to relaxation and well-being.


A skylight installation is an excellent option for homeowners who want to improve the lighting and ventilation in their homes. From the physical and psychological benefits of natural light to energy savings and improved indoor air quality, skylights offer a range of advantages that can help to make your home a brighter, healthier, and more comfortable place to live.


Types of Skylights

Skylights are an excellent way to add natural light and ventilation to your home, but with so many types of skylights available, choosing the right one for your needs can take time and effort.


Fixed skylights: Non-operable for added Natural Light and a View

Fixed skylights are the simplest type of skylight designed to provide additional natural light and a view of the sky. As the name suggests, these skylights are non-operable, meaning they cannot be opened. This makes them a good choice for areas that do not require ventilation, such as attics or areas where ventilation is already provided through other means.


Fixed skylights are available in various sizes and shapes, including square, rectangular, circular, and even custom shapes. They can also be installed with unique glazing options, such as tempered or laminated glass, to increase energy efficiency and reduce glare.

Ventilating skylights: Operable for added Natural Light and Fresh Air

As the name suggests, ventilation skylights are designed to provide natural light and fresh air to your home. These skylights can be opened and closed, allowing you to control the ventilation and light entering your home.


There are two main types of ventilating skylights: manual and electric. A hand crank or a pole operates manual skylights, while a remote control operates electric skylights. Both types of skylights are available in various sizes and shapes and can be installed with unique glazing options to increase energy efficiency and reduce noise.


Tubular skylights: Small and Affordable, Ideal for Dark or Cramped Spaces

Tubular skylights, also known as sun tunnels, are a small and affordable option for adding natural light to dark or cramped spaces in your home. These skylights consist of a small dome on the roof, which captures natural light and channels it through a reflective tube to a diffuser in the ceiling.


Tubular skylights are ideal for spaces where a larger skylight is impractical, such as closets, hallways, or bathrooms. They are available in various sizes and shapes and can be installed with unique diffusers to control the amount of light that enters your home.


Factors to Consider

When considering a skylight installation for your home, there are several factors to consider to ensure you choose the right skylight for your needs. We will discuss the four main elements: location, size, glazing, and style.


Location: Optimal Placement for Maximum Light and Ventilation

The location of your skylight is critical to its effectiveness in providing natural light and ventilation to your home. To maximize natural light, the skylight should be placed on your home's roof, which will receive the most direct sunlight. Additionally, consider putting the skylight where it can draw in cooler air and vent out warmer air to improve ventilation.


Size: Choosing the right size for your Space

Choosing the right size for your skylight is crucial to ensuring that it provides the right amount of natural light and ventilation for your space. Smaller a skylight may need to provide more light, while too large a skylight may let in too much heat, causing your home to become too warm in the summer. Consider the size of the room and the amount of natural light you want to let in when choosing the size of your skylight.

Glazing: Choosing the Correct Type of Glass for Your Skylight


The type of glass you choose for your skylight can have a significant impact on its energy efficiency, as well as its ability to filter out harmful UV rays and reduce glare. Several types of glazing are available, including tempered glass, laminated glass, and low-emissivity (Low-E) glass. Consider the climate in your area and the orientation of your skylight when choosing the right type of glazing for your skylight.

Style: Choosing a Skylight that Complements Your Home's Design

Lastly, you'll want to choose a skylight that complements the style and design of your home. Skylights come in various shapes and styles, including rectangular, circular, and even custom shapes. Consider the architectural style of your home and the color and material of your roof when choosing a skylight that will seamlessly blend in with your home's design.


When considering a skylight installation for your home, consider the location, size, glazing, and style of your skylight to ensure that you choose a skylight that will provide the right amount of natural light and ventilation while complementing the design of your home. With the right skylight, you can enhance the beauty and comfort of your home while enjoying the benefits of natural light and fresh air.


Skylight Installation Process

Skylights are an excellent addition to any home, providing natural light and ventilation to any room. Knowing the installation process is essential if you're considering a skylight installation.


Preparing for installation: Measuring and Cutting the Opening

Before installing the skylight, the first step is to prepare the roof for the building. This involves measuring and cutting the opening for the skylight. The opening should be the same size as the skylight frame and be in the optimal position for natural light and ventilation.


Once the opening is cut, installing a curb around the opening is essential to raise the skylight frame off the roof's surface. This curb also helps to prevent any water leaks.


Installing the skylight: Securing the Skylight and Flashing it

Once the curb is installed, it's time to install the skylight. Depending on the manufacturer's instructions, the skylight is secured to the curb with screws or bolts. The next step is installing flashing around the skylight to prevent water leaks. The flashing is usually made of metal and is installed in layers around the skylight frame.


Finishing touches: Insulating and Sealing the Skylight

The final step in the skylight installation process is to insulate and seal the skylight. This helps to prevent heat loss during the winter and heat gain during the summer. The skylight is insulated using expanding foam or fiberglass insulation, then sealed with caulking or sealant to prevent air leaks.


It's important to note that skylight installation can be a complex process and may require the help of a professional installer. A professional installer will ensure that the skylight is installed correctly and any potential issues, such as water leaks or heat loss, are addressed.


The skylight installation process involves preparing the roof, installing the skylight, and adding the finishing touches. It's essential to take the necessary steps to ensure that the skylight is installed correctly to prevent future issues. With a professionally installed skylight, you can enjoy the benefits of natural light and ventilation in your home for years.


Maintenance and Care

Skylights are an excellent addition to any home, providing natural light and ventilation to any room. However, like any other part of your home, skylights require regular maintenance and care to ensure their longevity and optimal performance.


Regular cleaning: Keeping your Skylight Free of Dirt and Debris

Regular cleaning is essential to keep your skylight free of dirt and debris that can accumulate over time. Dirt and debris can block natural light and impede proper ventilation, making a living less comfortable. Use a non-abrasive cleaner and a soft cloth or sponge to clean your skylight. Avoid using abrasive materials or harsh chemicals that can damage the skylight's surface or sealant.


Inspecting for damage: Identifying and Repairing any Leaks or Damage

Inspecting your skylight regularly for any signs of damage or leaks is essential. Check for cracks or breaks in the glass or frame and look for water stains or wear around the skylight. If you notice any damage or leaks, it's essential to address them promptly to prevent further damage to your home. Small cracks or leaks can often be repaired with a sealant or patch, but larger damage may require the help of a professional installer.


Professional maintenance: Scheduling Annual Check-ups with a Certified Installer

Professional maintenance is an essential part of skylight care. A certified installer can inspect your skylight for any signs of wear and tear and make necessary repairs or adjustments. They can also clean and lubricate any mechanical parts, ensuring your skylight operates smoothly and efficiently. Scheduling an annual check-up with a certified installer can prevent potential problems and extend the life of your skylight.


Installing a skylight in your home can bring numerous benefits, including increased natural light, improved indoor air quality, energy efficiency, and a mood boost. However, there are also factors to consider, such as the location, size, glazing, and style of the skylight. The installation involves:

  • Measuring and cutting the opening.
  • Securing and flashing the skylight.
  • Insulating and sealing it.

Maintaining and caring for your skylight ensures its longevity and optimal performance. Regular cleaning, inspecting for damage, and scheduling professional maintenance can prevent potential problems and extend the life of your skylight.


We encourage you to take the next step towards a brighter, healthier home by considering the installation of a skylight. Consult with a certified installer to help you choose the right type of skylight for your home and to ensure proper installation and maintenance. With the benefits of natural light and ventilation, a skylight can improve the comfort and well-being of your home for years to come.
